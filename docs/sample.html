Project Aurora — Concept Brief

Project Aurora is a proposed initiative to redesign the city's public wayfinding system. The existing signage network, installed in 2004, has reached end-of-life and no longer reflects the city's expanded transit routes, new cultural districts, or accessibility requirements.

This brief outlines the project scope, timeline, and key stakeholders for review by the City Planning Commission.

Objectives

  • Replace all 340 existing wayfinding signs across the downtown core
  • Extend coverage to the three new transit corridors opened since 2019
  • Meet WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards for contrast, font size, and tactile elements
  • Integrate QR codes linking to real-time transit and walking directions

Background

The current wayfinding system was designed by Meridian Studio as part of the 2004 Downtown Revitalization Plan. While effective at the time, several factors have rendered it inadequate:

The existing signs reference landmarks and routes that no longer exist. Visitors frequently report confusion at intersections where signs point to the "Convention Center" — demolished in 2018 — or the "Waterfront Shuttle" — discontinued in 2021.

A 2024 pedestrian survey found that 62% of respondents rated the current wayfinding system as "unhelpful" or "confusing," up from 28% in a comparable 2015 survey.

Phased Rollout

Design Principles

Clarity
Every sign must be interpretable within 5 seconds by a first-time visitor with no local knowledge.
Consistency
Uniform visual language across all sign types — directional, informational, and regulatory.
Durability
Materials rated for 15-year outdoor lifespan with minimal maintenance in a marine climate.

Key Stakeholders

The project requires coordination across multiple city departments and external partners. The steering committee will include representatives from Transportation, Public Works, the Accessibility Advisory Board, and the Downtown Business Association.

Community engagement sessions are planned for April and May 2026 to gather public input on design prototypes before fabrication begins.

A public comment period will run from June 1–30, 2026 via the Project Aurora feedback portal.
